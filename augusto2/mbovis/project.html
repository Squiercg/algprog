Objective

Develop a traceability platform for Bovine tuberculosis in South America, providing up-to-date information about the disease evolution.

More specific

  • Increase the number of sequenced genomes from Mycobacterium bovis isolates from Brasil, Argentina, Uruguai and Paraguai, from different regions and hosts.
  • Mount and annotate these M. Bovis Genomes
  • Evaluate the genetic polymorphism from south-America isolates and compare them with north-American isolates (partnership with NADC/ARS/ USDA)
  • Compare the south-Americans isolates genomes with Mycobacterium tuberculosis (CMT) reference strains.
  • Compare phylogeny approaches for M. bovis, using as template sequenced genomes and meta-data about them, analyzing the presence of single-nucleotide polymorphism (SNPs), orthologs protein families and ortologs protein families with snps.
  • Based on machine learning models and patterns recognition, define which phylogeny approach promote stronger associations between clusters found and genome meta-data
  • Define a minimum set of genes/protein families that give robustness to the selected model thought artificial neural network models.
  • Evaluate the phylogeography and spread of M. bovis thought ancestral reconstruction models
  • Develop a web platform for Bovine tuberculosis traceability, from which it is possible to understand evolution features from this disease, in special at south America, also, be able to easily upgrade this understand while new genomes are added to our database
  • Develop a mobile platform with part of the information about this disease and its traceability
